Eternity is a movie with a great premise and a simple but solid message. It mixes rom-com tropes with the philosophical questioning of sci-fi short stories in a way that evokes The Good Place.

Joan faces a bit of a cliche decision between her two husbands in the afterlife. The movie tricks you into thinking that Joan will make a single choice at the end of the movie. Instead, it takes a much more roundabout method to add some nuance to the conclusion.

In doing so it tries to cast both husbands in both good and bad lights. Ultimately I think it doesn't do a perfect job of this. Rather than feeling like human failings, the downsides of each husband are more exaggerated and feel insensitive. It could be argued that under the stress of their situation their reactions are actually quite human, but personally I think the movie could have painted them in this gray in a way that left room for more redemption in the end. Instead, the criticism of Larry is sort of left unaddressed, and for Luke his happiness is not certain enough at the end.

That being said, the type of narrative that they are constructing here is really unique, and I give it points for trying something new and doing it well, even if not perfectly.

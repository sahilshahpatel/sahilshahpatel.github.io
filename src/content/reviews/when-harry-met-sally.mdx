I had heard a lot about this movie before watching it, of course. I knew it took place over years and I had heard the central question is "can men and women be friends" (more on that later). Given what I knew, I expected to like this movie and, well, I did! I should note that I did watch it as part of a date, though I don't think that colored my thoughts too strongly.

Let me start with what I didn't like and move into the good stuff. First, something I didn't love throughout the movie was how many important facts are just implied. I actually liked the sort of style of it all, but there were quite a few that went over my head. The first and worst was right at the beginning. I did not understand who Harry and Sally were to each other at all, or why they would be in the car for so long. This is almost certainly just something that changed with the times. I bet if you watched this movie when it came out, it would be clear that they knew each other through Amanda and were driving together for company, to save gas, because only one had a car, or whatever reason really. It's just that in 2026 it is just not common to move with basically a stranger for any reason. Similarly, it took me a bit to understand that Marie was seeing a married man. And as the time flashed forwards it wasn't obvious if people were still in their previous relationship or not. Ultimately I'm ok with a lot of this ambiguity, and most of it you can figure out easily enough, but overall I felt there was a bit too much.

The second hard part of the film was how unlikeable Harry is. Now this will transition into what I did like, because Harry's transformation is pretty good. But there is still a base layer of sexism to Harry's opinions and while he appears to change, the misogyny is never directly addressed. In the 90s that was probably good enough, but with 2026 cultural context, it felt like a little bit was missing there.

But let's start talking about the good parts! The series of vignettes was great, and I especially loved how they intercut it with the documentary-style love stories. That perfectly broke up the time skips and also subtly tells the audience that this movie is a little less about the specific plot of our main characters than average and a little more about the overall idea of love and what it can be.

I was told that the reason they included the fake orgasm scene was because they felt Sally was too boring. Personally I didn't feel that was the case, but maybe that's just because I related more strongly to her and so felt that as the audience insert she didn't need to be as flashy or unique. As a fan of a slow burn romance, I thought that the both of them played into it fairly well over the course of the movie.

Ok so at the end of it all, can men and women be friends? Well, I don't think that's actually the point of the movie at all. In the specific case of Harry and Sally, they grew from friends to lovers. But I don't think that was inevitable necessarily. And really the focal character arc of the movie is Harry growing into a more mature person. So to me, Harry learns that friendship is absolutely possible, and enjoys that friendship for many years before the romance develops. In another world, perhaps they would have remained friends the whole time. And so in looking at their specific story, we see one way things could unfold, but not all the ways.

Overall, this was a pretty great movie that feels its age a little bit, but not in the most important ways.

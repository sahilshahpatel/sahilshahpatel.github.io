I'll write this short review without spoiling the twist, so feel free to read on! If you know the famous line from this movie, you still do not know the twist and you should absolutely go watch it!

I watched The Sixth Sense for the first time a long while back. I wish I could remember exactly how I reacted to the twist or if I saw it coming, but unfortunately I don't. But I was lucky enough to watch it for a second time with a group of friends that included four people who hadn't seen it before and did not know the twist! On my first watch I remember thinking the movie was good, but I don't recall it being amazing. On my second watch that completely changed. To get a full S or S+ rating a movie often has to be watched at the right time when it's going to have its maximum emotional effect. That didn't happen for me with The Sixth Sense, but it will still well above and beyond most of the good movies I watch, and so an S- just feels right.

One thing I praise Knives Out for is how rewarding it makes a second watch. There are so many small details which make the twist obvious in retrospect but impossible to notice in real time. The Sixth Sense does this even better in my opinion. In a murder mystery it makes some sense that there are going to be clues that you miss the first time, and so you're basically expecting that to be true and watching with intent to discover as many of those as you can. This movie, though, is operating as a drama in many ways, and so you aren't primed to think like that. In fact, the movie is a great movie even without the twist! It has everything you expect out of a drama with a single main character and their narrative arc. When you finally understand the twist a second character is suddenly elevated to main character status and a narrative arc worth its own separate movie appears out of nowhere. It's that magic trick that earns this movie its spot in the S tier.

The Pitt is one of the best and most emotional shows I have ever seen. Unlike some of my other S+ tier media like Arcane or Andor, The Pitt is both realistic fiction and focuses less on societal issues and more on deeply personal ones. I love societal commentary (and the Pitt has some of it), but I think the bedrock of any good story is grounded and personal character arcs, and The Pitt has that in spades.

I don't generally like medical dramas, mostly because I'm quite squeamish. As this show was described to me, I debated even watching it because I knew it would be somewhat graphic. But I kept hearing about how amazing it was, and also how good a portrayal of an emergency room it was. In the end, there were plenty of moments where I had to look away, but it was so worth it to experience the expert weaving of narratives that this show displays.

The Pitt supports so many different stories at the same time. There is Dr. Robbie, the attending, who is suffering from PTSD after Covid. Through him we see the toll it takes on a person to work day in and day out in the ER. There is Dana Evans who has a similar conflict but processes it a different way, and who also shows you how important the role of nurses in the ER. Through Dr. Mohan we debate what bedside manner should mean and the individualized strengths and weaknesses of doctors. Through Dr. Collins, Dr. McKay, and Dr. King we see how doctors themselves may be dealing with medical things in their own lives and how they navigate that extra personal attachment in the workplace. Through the students we get our own myriad of stories including the overcompensating jerk, the child prodigy, and the naive small-town misfit. Even in that long list I did not cover every storyline, and I didn't even mention the storylines that focus less on individual characters and more on the hospital as a whole. Obviously some stories take precedence over others, but all of them are well written and well supported in way that is truly remarkable.

I don't know how else to give this show the praise that it deserves. It's absolutely heart-wrenching, and it embodies empathy as it gives you a window into the deepest crevasses of these people's lives, all over the course of a single day. This show moved me so much emotionally that it could not possibly earn less than an S+.

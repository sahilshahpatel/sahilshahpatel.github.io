Writing a feminist fantasy is a challenge, and I think The Once and Future Witches used a very unique story structure to try and strike it from another angle. In this review will be several comparisons to When Women Were Dragons, and to start I'll say I liked this book more.

On its own, this story is entertaining and gripping. The characters feel quite real, and even as much as they are literally archetypes, they also feel nuanced and human. I loved the decision to have three main characters and therefore three very distinct and yet similarly fleshed out ideas of femininity, resistance, and perseverance. The side characters throughout this story also add depth rather than feeling like a distraction. For example, there is ample talk of race and a smaller reference to transgenderism which make the world feel larger, not stretched thin. The one angle I felt did not do that was the internalized misogyny. Grace Wiggin and even Mrs. Stone were given too large of roles for how little development their perspectives got, in my opinion. And I also felt that the story was too kind to them, though that is my personal opinion.

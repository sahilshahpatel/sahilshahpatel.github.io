- physical book has short stories in the appendix which I'm sad I missed!
- YA fiction focuses on main characters as stand-ins for the readers. Leaves less room for complex villains who would distract from that
- The pacing is good from a plot perspective
- The pacing of the exposition doesn't match the pacing of the plot. The beginning makes you really intrigued about the world, but then the plot itself pushes you through each world much too fast to be able to really learn about them
- The premise of this book and the initial exposition is SO GOOD, and that's really hard to do. But the direction Schwab chose to take it, especially in the end just didn't deliver on the promises of the first. We're set up to see White London as a potential adversary but not the main villain -- that's supposed to be Black London. We're set up to think that both Lila and Kell returning to their homelands was going to be a major conflict to be resolved. And we're set up wonder about the mystery of Holland. None of those things really have good resolutions.
- Barron was a very well written character. He's obviously a very minor character, but his few actions and few words characterize him extremely well. And most of all his character really is just a way to tell the story of Lilah indirectly, which it also does extremely well.

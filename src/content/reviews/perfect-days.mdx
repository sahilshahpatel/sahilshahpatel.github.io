If you read the premise of this movie, you'll know what you're in for. Hirayama cleans public toilets in Japan for a living, and this movie is his experiences through a week or so. In this time we get a glimpse of his routine and how he finds joy in the simple moments in life. Then we are introduced to new characters, and through each we see the struggles and pain that life can offer. By the end you come to understand Hirayama's philosophy of life.

The movie is just lovely. It's largely without dialogue at the beginning, just allowing you to sit in the ambience it creates. Then, as you become accustomed to that atmosphere, it breaks it in ways that challenge your understanding. The film is beautifully put together to tell the story it aims to tell.

My one gripe with the movie is its ending. And I actually think the concept of the ending is basically perfect. I just didn't love the actor's performance, which is crazy because he's amazing in the rest of the film. I have extremely small complaints but they somehow stacked up to make me just not believe that final moment. For one, his hands just move around the wheel too much. And more importantly, the expressions that cross his face and the order in which it happens just doesn't make much sense to me. Rather than feeling like I'm watching a character process the emotions of all the days we just saw throughout the movie, I felt like I was watching and actor trying to play this emotional moment without really having determined exactly what the character is feeling and why.

I don't want to end on a bad note though. That one complaint does not stop the film from producing the effect it desires. The story it seeks to tell is a beautiful one, and it's beautifully told.

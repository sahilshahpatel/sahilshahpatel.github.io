— About Me —

I am currently a Sophomore studying Computer Engineering at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign. I'm involved in multiple ACM special interests groups including SIGMusic and SIGGraph where I am a co-chair and a project lead respectively. As part of UIUC's Reflections|Projections conference, I am also the game engine lead for MechMania, an AI hackathon. My interests are varied and include audio, game development, signal processing, cybersecurity, and machine learning.

— Work Experience —

Nokia: R&D Intern Summer 2019

This past summer I had the opportunity to intern at Nokia on the 5G and Small Cells team. Under the guidance of my mentor, I worked with the fault-correction team on three main projects:

  • I performed data analysis on fault correction efficiency to try to find the root cause of large time-to-closure faults. After processing thousands of unlabeled records, we were able to establish some thresholds which generally indicated fault correction issues. However, we ultimately decided that the best solution moving forward would be to add a satisfaction label to our fault correction records to better enable future data analysis.
  • I developed a mobile solution for data access to an on-premise database for Nokia sales representatives. Using Microsoft Power BI and Visual Basic scripts, I automated the entire data update and sync processes on a virtual machine.
  • I conducted virtual tests for an in-office 4G/5G lab site to assist developers in detecting a bug which had been reported by customers in the field.
